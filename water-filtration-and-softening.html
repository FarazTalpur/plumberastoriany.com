Frequently Asked Questions

Water filtration removes contaminants, sediments, and chemicals from water through various types of filters. Water softening specifically targets hardness in water by removing minerals like calcium and magnesium that cause scale buildup.

The frequency of replacing water filters depends on the type and model, but it's generally recommended to replace them every 2 to 6 months. Always refer to the manufacturer's instructions for specific guidance.

Water softeners prevent scale buildup in plumbing systems and appliances, which can improve efficiency and longevity. They also help in producing softer skin and hair and prevent spots on dishes and glassware.

Yes, softened water is safe to drink. However, the sodium content in softened water might be a concern for those on low-sodium diets. In such cases, consider using a separate drinking water filter.

Common types of water filtration systems include activated carbon filters, reverse osmosis systems, UV disinfection filters, and mechanical filters. Each has its own set of advantages, depending on the specific contaminants present in your water.
