Frequently Asked Questions

The tools needed typically include a drill, adjustable wrench, tube cutter, Teflon tape, and a water line kit appropriate for your ice maker model.

Identify the nearest cold water pipe, often found under the kitchen sink or behind a refrigerator, which is typically closest to the ice maker installation location.

A copper or braided stainless steel water line is recommended due to their durability and resistance to bursting as opposed to plastic lines.

Using a water filter is highly recommended as it improves the taste and clarity of the ice by removing impurities and sediment from the water supply.

Turn off the water supply immediately, inspect the line for any visible damage or loose connections, and ensure all fittings are tightly secured. If the issue persists, consult a professional plumber.
